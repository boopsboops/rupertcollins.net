Rupert A. Collins

Research and scientific interests

My primary research interest is with understanding patterns in local and global biodiversity, and particularly for freshwater fishes. A brief summary of some of my broader research interests is outlined below:
DNA barcoding and bioinformatics
Using DNA barcodes as a tool for biodiversity assessment and regulatory applications (e.g. monitoring trade in ornamental fishes).
Investigating quantitative techniques for biodiversity assessment (e.g. GMYC).
Developing new approaches for data analysis with the R package Spider (Species Identity and Evolution in R).
Biodiversity informatics (iPhylo).
Improving standards in DNA barcoding, and critiquing commonly used methods.
Taxonomy and systematics
Describing species and integrating DNA data into taxonomic practice (Padial et al., 2010).
Assessing congruence and conflict between morphological and molecular datasets (Cruickshank, 2011).
Phylogenetics and evolution
Phylogenetic hypothesis testing using multilocus data.
Comparative methods (e.g. Ape, phytools).
Molecular clock estimation for estimating evolutionary patterns in diversification.
Biogeography, phylogeography and speciation.
Ecology and conservation
Assessment of biodiversity, ecology and trophic linkages using eDNA techniques (Ficetola et al., 2008).
Human impacts and invasive species.
